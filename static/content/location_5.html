Burke and Hare are walking back to Hare's house. They are walking past a pub. The men slow down.

BURKE... Hare, have you got a match?

HARE... No I don't. Had to give up my smokes when Jim didn't pay me for a couple months. Have you got one spare?

BURKE... Sure do. Here you go. I'll find us a match.

Hare walks over to the pub and approaches someone leaving the pub.

BURKE... Got a spare match?... Thanks.

Burke lights his cigarette and returns to Hare.

BURKE... Take a light off mine.

Hare lights his cigarette from Burke's cigarette and inhales.

HARE... Money's good for the lungs!

BURKE... You're right brother! Where's our next body coming from then? Needs to be fresh.

HARE... We need to pick someone nobody will miss, someone nobody will even notice is gone...

Burke and Hare's conversation is cut off by the sound of skuffling and smashing glass. They freeze and listen intently

HARE... Did you hear that?

BURKE... Let's take a closer look.

HARE... Sounds like it came from the back of the pub by the stairs. Come on.

Burke and Hare sneak up to the top of the stairs.

HARE... It's just a bum.

Burke and Hare look at each other again...

BURKE... Let me do the honours.

Burke shoves the BUM down the stairs. The Bum yells out as he tumbles to the bottom. His cries are silenced by several cracking sounds and he lands at the bottom mute.

BURKE... Well that was easy.

HARE... Great work Burke. Let's get us some more money.

Burke and Hare pass slowly and quietly through the eerie streets of Edinburgh, heaving the body through the tunnel with them as they did with the 13 other victims which followed this murder.
